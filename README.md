## Retro City Defender (CRT Edition)
A high-octane 2D action-defense homage to the golden age of arcade gaming. Originally inspired by the classic concepts of Dave Theurer, this version has been "vibe coded" by 220 and Google's Gemini to bring a modern tactical edge to a retro aesthetic.

### 🚀 Introduction
The world is under an escalating aerial bombardment. As the commander of the central defensive battery, you are the last line of defense for ten thriving urban centers. Your objective is simple but grueling: survive the waves, manage your limited resources, and ensure the survival of your people.
Featuring a simulated CRT interface with authentic scanlines and synthesized audio, Retro City Defender captures the grit and tension of 80s defense systems.

### 🕹️ Pilot's Manual
#### 1. Basic Controls
Aiming: Move your Mouse to track incoming threats. The central cannon will follow your movements.
50mm Autocannon (Left Click): Rapid-fire kinetic rounds. These shells travel through targets and are your primary tool for shredding fast-moving drones.
Homing Missiles (Right Click): High-impact explosive ordnance. These missiles automatically track the threat closest to your cursor.
Note: You only have 10 Missiles per round. Use them wisely.

#### 2. Threat Assessment
Missiles (White): Fast-moving kinetic projectiles. High resistance to 50mm fire; Homing Missiles are recommended for instant interception.
Planes (Purple): Strategic bombers. They fly high and drop heavy payloads. Intercept them before they release their bombs.
Drones (Yellow): Erratic, fast-moving nuisances. Easily dispatched with the 50mm Autocannon.
Bombs (Red): Heavy damage payloads dropped by planes. They are fragile but devastating if they reach a city.

#### 3. Mission Flow
The Wave: Each round introduces a new wave of threats. Difficulty scales as enemy speed and numbers increase.
Intermission: Once a wave is cleared, non-destroyed cities will undergo emergency repairs (40% HP recovery), and your missile silos will be replenished to maximum capacity.
Defeat: The mission ends when all ten cities are reduced to rubble.

### 🛠️ Technical Specs
Visuals: 60FPS Canvas rendering with CRT scanline and flicker simulation.
Audio: Real-time synthesized Web Audio (Square, Sawtooth, and White Noise oscillators).
Engine: Pure HTML5/JavaScript.
